## Problem Statement: A time-sensitive courier making multiple deliveries across a city needs an app that intelligently plans and reorders delivery stops in real time, allowing them to avoid traffic delays, missed deadlines, and unnecessary backtracking.

## Product: Apple Maps

Heuristics: 7 of the 10 Usability Heuristics (https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/)

Severity Ratings: (https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-to-rate-the-severity-of-usability-problems/)

0 = I don't agree that this is a usability problem at all
1 = Cosmetic problem only: need not be fixed unless extra time is available on project
2 = Minor usability problem: fixing this should be given low priority
3 = Major usability problem: important to fix, so should be given high priority
4 = Usability catastrophe: imperative to fix this before product can be released

### 1: Visibility of System Status -

Analysis: One of the great things about this product is that is will notify you of any traffic delays, faster routes, weather updates or even accidents that have occurred in certain areas. However, there are times when the system is not updated to align with the activity in real time. Example being, an accident could have happened 2 hours ago, but it will sometimes have a glitch in the system that shows it as current. There is a feature that gives the user the opportunity to state whether or not the accident is still there, but in my opinion it should not be the users job to do that. Especially if they are driving.

Rating: 2

### 2: Match Between the System and the Real World -

Analysis: The success of this product is contingent on it being able to accomplish this heuristic. It does a fine job of being a mirror of the world and the surroundings. From different map viewpoints (Satellite, Explore, Transit, Driving) to easily understood text such as "Home" and "Work" that you can easily store those locations in. Visually the maps are easy to read but if I would make one improvement I would make the text a bit larger on the locations in the map for those who are hard of seeing or at enable a feature that allows you to adjust the font size.

Rating: 1

### 3: User Control and Freedom -

Analysis: There have been times when I have been using the product and entered the wrong address while creating a route, what I love about the product is that it allows you to remove the address(s) if need be. However, when creating a route with 2 stops or more, it is not apparent off of first glance how to do this. I believe this visually can be improved by placing a small icon to the left of the address to make it clear that the action is possible. It could be a small x or even a red dot with a line going through it horizontally, something would be better than nothing.

Rating: 1

### 4: Consistency and Standards -

Analysis: Everything is clear and concise when it comes to navigation, text, and imagery. Apple maps sets the standard for what a simple and easy to use maps app should be. I believe it is visually appealing as well as pleasant to the user because everything is simply laid out for you and doesn't require to much cognitive load.

Rating: 0

### 5: Error Prevention -

Analysis: The only real error I have seen and experienced with this product is that there are times it will take you to the wrong location. It may be a street over and because the satellite can't read an area you may end up just minutes away from the correct location. This is where Google Maps wins. Though visually Apple Maps is more appealing, I have to say Google Maps is more accurate. This is something that must be fixed in order to enhance the product. Google will have representatives come out and scan areas, even remote locations annually. Maybe Apple can learn from those strategies and other practices that are being implemented at Google to improve this feature.

Rating: 4

### 6: Recognition Rather than Recall -

Analysis: The goal of this guideline is to ensure the information that is on one interface to the next is easily remembered and includes clear information that is required to use the design, or product. Apple maps does this by adding in placeholders, labels, names of landmarks in all areas, as well as elements and icons that symbolize what things are. Such as the pin emoji to represent a location, fork icons to represent food locations, and gas pump icons to represent gas stations.

Rating: 0

### 7: Aesthetic and Minimalist Design -

Analysis: This product does a fine job at visually displaying elements in a way to where you don't have to think much, the journey is there for you to simply partake in vs create. Once you put in a location, it takes you to a page of different routes you can take to get to that location, once you pick the route, it will tell you how long it takes to get there, how many miles, and the speed limit. All in a way to where the information isn't cluttered or arranged chaotically. Apple Maps by far has the most aesthetically pleasing design.

Rating: 0

## Key Takeaways:

When it comes to an easy to use GPS, that can be used to arrange routes for couriers, Apple maps is a great option visually, but not a great one for functionality.

There are some inconsistencies that can be improved on. I would propose creating a product that can do the route mapping for you, and update the route in real time if there are any delays and any other traffic notifications that are critical for drivers.

While Apple Maps gives you the ability to map your own route out, you still have to do it manually. Which can be tedious.

My vision for a more improved experience would be a product that only needs the addresses, and then will automatically sort out the locations by itself based off of distance and traffic, in order to get the quickest route without having to do the mapping yourself.
